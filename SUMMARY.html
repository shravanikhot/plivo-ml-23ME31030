End-of-Turn Detection Plivo AI/ML Track

Roll no: 23ME31030 — Approach summary, honest scoring, and what I'd do next.

The task

Predict, at every silence pause in a user's turn, the probability p_eot that the turn is truly over — using only audio before the pause starts (causality constraint). Scored by mean response delay at ≤5% false-cutoff rate, against a silence-only baseline of ~1600 ms.

Approach

I extended the causal feature extractor in features.py with prosodic cues grounded in how speech naturally signals turn completion:

  • Pitch (F0) behavior: final voiced pitch relative to the speaker's own median, and F0 slope over the last voiced run — statements tend to fall in pitch; continuations often stay level or rise.
  • Final-syllable lengthening: duration of the last voiced run relative to the turn's average voiced-run duration.
  • Energy decay: short-term energy slope and final-frame energy relative to the segment mean.
  • Voicing ratio & trailing silence: how much of the pre-pause window was voiced vs. silent.
  • Turn-history features: number, mean, and std of already-completed prior pauses in the same turn (legally causal — their pause_end occurred before the current pause's pause_start).
  • Speaking rate over the whole turn so far, and the pause's position in time.

These 12 features feed a GradientBoostingClassifier (150 trees, depth 2, lr 0.08), trained on English + Hindi pauses combined so the model generalizes across languages (the hidden test set is mostly Hindi).

A mistake I caught — and corrected

Overfitting via data leakage on the "final" refit. My training script does a proper held-out-turn split internally for a sanity check, but then refits on all data before saving the model (matching how you'd actually ship a final model). When I first scored predict.py's output, I was scoring the model on the same turns it had just been refit on — giving an inflated AUC of 0.93–0.95 and a delay of ~600 ms.

I re-evaluated on a proper held-out split (25% of turns, grouped by turn_id so no turn's pauses leak across the split) that the model never saw during fitting. That gave the honest numbers below — meaningfully worse than the inflated ones, but still a real, decisive win over baseline.

Why the score plateaus where it does

Held-out turn accuracy (0.641) is only modestly above chance (0.597). With ~250 pauses per language and 12 features, the model has limited data to learn subtle prosodic distinctions, and prosody alone can't fully resolve genuinely ambiguous pauses — e.g. a speaker trailing off with falling pitch mid-thought looks identical, acoustically, to a real ending. Closing this gap likely needs either more training turns, or a different signal entirely (see below).

What I'd do with one more day

Add a lexical/semantic completion signal — e.g. a lightweight causal model scoring whether the transcript-so-far reads like a syntactically complete utterance — combined with the existing prosodic features. Prosody says how something was said; completion-likelihood says whether it needs to say more, and the two are complementary. I'd also gather more training turns per language to reduce the variance visible in the held-out accuracy.
